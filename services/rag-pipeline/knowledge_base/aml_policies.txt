ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING AND COUNTER-TERRORIST FINANCING POLICY
FinShield AI Internal Compliance Reference
(Based on PMLA 2002, FATF Recommendations, RBI AML Master Direction 2023)

SECTION 1: MONEY LAUNDERING STAGES AND DETECTION

1.1 Placement Stage
The initial introduction of illicit funds into the financial system.
Detection indicators:
- Large cash deposits inconsistently sized
- Multiple deposits just below CTR threshold (structuring / smurfing)
- Rapid deposit followed by immediate wire transfer
- Deposits from multiple geographic locations on same day

1.2 Layering Stage
Complex series of transactions to distance funds from source.
Detection indicators:
- Multiple inter-account transfers within short period
- Round-trip transactions (A → B → C → A)
- Use of multiple payment channels in sequence (UPI → wallet → NEFT)
- Transactions through multiple jurisdictions
- Mule account networks (fan-out patterns)

1.3 Integration Stage
Re-introduction of laundered funds into legitimate economy.
Detection indicators:
- Sudden large purchases from previously low-activity accounts
- Investment in high-value goods (jewellery, electronics)
- Real estate transactions from digital wallet accounts
- Merchant settlements to newly opened accounts

SECTION 2: SPECIFIC RED FLAGS FOR DIGITAL PAYMENTS

2.1 UPI-Specific Red Flags
- More than 15 UPI transactions per hour from same VPA
- Transactions to/from VPAs registered less than 7 days ago
- Same device used across multiple different UPI accounts
- Transactions at exact interval times (bot signature)
- Collection requests from unknown VPAs for large amounts

2.2 Wallet-Specific Red Flags
- Wallet loaded near maximum limit repeatedly in short period
- Immediate transfer of wallet balance after loading
- Multiple wallets operated from same device
- Wallet-to-wallet transfers across many accounts (layering chain)

2.3 Merchant Payment Red Flags
- Merchant receiving unusually high volume relative to category benchmark
- High refund-to-sales ratio (> 15%)
- Merchant and customer sharing same device fingerprint
- Transactions at consistent times suggesting automation
- Merchant operating from residential address (shell business)

SECTION 3: MULE ACCOUNT DETECTION

3.1 Definition
A mule account is a bank/wallet account used to receive and forward illicit funds, often by an unknowing third party recruited via social media or job scams.

3.2 Behavioural Indicators
- Account receives multiple small transfers (₹5K-₹9,999) within short period
- Funds immediately transferred onward (< 2 hours of receipt)
- Account holder shows no typical consumer spending patterns
- Account primarily used for pass-through rather than own consumption
- Multiple mule accounts receiving from same source (fan-out detected via graph analysis)

3.3 Recommended Actions
- File STR with FIU-IND within 7 days of identification
- Freeze account pending investigation
- Notify CERT-In if cyber crime suspected
- Document social media recruitment evidence if available

SECTION 4: TRANSACTION MONITORING THRESHOLDS

4.1 Automatic Alert Thresholds
| Scenario                        | Threshold              | Action          |
|--------------------------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| Single cash transaction         | > ₹10,00,000           | CTR to FIU-IND  |
| Structuring (multiple txns)    | Total > ₹10L/day       | STR             |
| International transfer          | Any amount             | Enhanced review |
| High-risk merchant              | > ₹50,000 single txn   | Manual review   |
| New beneficiary transfer        | > ₹25,000              | Cooling period  |
| Dormant account revival         | Any large txn          | Step-up auth    |

4.2 Velocity-Based Alerts
- 10+ transactions in 15 minutes: Medium risk, monitor
- 20+ transactions in 15 minutes: High risk, step-up authentication
- 50+ transactions in 30 minutes: Critical, suspend and investigate

SECTION 5: CUSTOMER RISK SCORING

5.1 Risk Factors
High-risk customer indicators:
- PEP (Politically Exposed Person) status
- High-risk country of origin or transaction destination
- Unusual business type (crypto, hawala, forex trading)
- Prior STR filings
- Device associated with known fraud accounts
- KYC inconsistencies (name/address mismatch)
- Account activity inconsistent with stated income/profession

5.2 Risk Tier Assignment
- Tier 1 (Low Risk): Verified KYC, consistent domestic transactions, known employer
- Tier 2 (Medium Risk): Incomplete KYC, occasional anomalies, cash-heavy transactions  
- Tier 3 (High Risk): PEP, high-risk country, prior flags, structuring patterns
- Tier 4 (Critical): Active STR, court order, sanctions list match

SECTION 6: SANCTIONS SCREENING

6.1 Mandatory Screening Lists
All transactions must be screened against:
- UN Security Council Consolidated List
- OFAC SDN List (US Treasury)
- EU Consolidated Sanctions List
- India PMLA Schedule (domestic politically exposed persons)
- Interpol Red Notice list (where available)

6.2 Screening Frequency
- Real-time screening for all transactions above ₹25,000
- Daily batch screening for all active accounts
- Immediate re-screening upon receipt of updated lists
