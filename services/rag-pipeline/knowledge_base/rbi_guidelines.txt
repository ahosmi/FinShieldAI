RBI GUIDELINES ON DIGITAL PAYMENT FRAUD PREVENTION
Master Circular — Customer Protection in Digital Transactions
(Reference: RBI/2023-24/73, RBI/2022-23/101)

SECTION 1: CUSTOMER LIABILITY IN UNAUTHORISED TRANSACTIONS

1.1 Zero Liability
A customer shall have zero liability where the deficiency is on part of the bank or payment system provider, irrespective of whether the transaction is reported by the customer or not.

1.2 Limited Liability
A customer shall have limited liability in cases involving third-party breach where the deficiency is neither on the part of the bank nor the customer, but lies elsewhere in the system, and the customer notifies the bank within 3 working days.

1.3 Transaction Reversal SLA
Banks must reverse/credit the amount in dispute within 10 working days of the customer reporting the unauthorised transaction.

SECTION 2: STEP-UP AUTHENTICATION REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Mandatory Two-Factor Authentication
All digital payment transactions above ₹5,000 must use two-factor authentication (2FA). First factor: device PIN or biometric. Second factor: OTP via registered mobile number.

2.2 Additional Verification Triggers
Banks and payment system operators must enforce additional step-up authentication for:
- Transactions from new devices not previously registered
- Transactions initiated from locations inconsistent with user's transaction history
- Transaction velocity exceeding 10 transactions in any 15-minute window
- Transactions from IP addresses flagged in the RBI's compromised IP registry
- Amounts exceeding 3x the user's 30-day average transaction amount

2.3 New Beneficiary Cooling Period
A mandatory 4-hour cooling period applies for first-time transactions to newly added beneficiaries for amounts exceeding ₹25,000.

SECTION 3: REAL-TIME FRAUD MONITORING OBLIGATIONS

3.1 Transaction Monitoring Systems
All payment system operators processing more than 1 million transactions per month must maintain a real-time transaction monitoring system capable of:
- Detecting velocity anomalies within 60 seconds
- Identifying geo-location inconsistencies
- Flagging device fingerprint anomalies
- Generating Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) within 7 days

3.2 Fraud Reporting to RBI
Payment system operators must report fraud incidents exceeding ₹1 lakh to RBI CSITE within 6 hours of detection. Monthly aggregate reports must be submitted to the RBI Fraud Monitoring Group (FMG).

3.3 Merchant Risk Categorisation
Merchants must be categorised by risk tier (Low/Medium/High) based on:
- Industry type (high-risk sectors: crypto, forex, gaming, pharmacy)
- Chargeback ratio (>1% = Medium, >2% = High)
- Refund-to-sales ratio
- Age of merchant account
- Geographic risk of operations

SECTION 4: ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING PROVISIONS

4.1 Reporting Threshold — Cash Transactions
All cash transactions above ₹10 lakh (₹10,00,000) must be reported as Cash Transaction Reports (CTRs) to FIU-IND.

4.2 Suspicious Transaction Reporting
STRs must be filed for:
- Structuring: multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds
- Round-tripping: funds sent and received back via different routes
- Unusual transaction patterns inconsistent with customer profile
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions (FATF grey/blacklist)

4.3 Customer Due Diligence
Enhanced KYC required for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- High-risk countries
- Accounts with unusual activity patterns post-onboarding

SECTION 5: REFUND AND CHARGEBACK CONTROLS

5.1 Refund Frequency Limits
Payment operators must implement controls to limit refund frequency:
- Maximum 3 refunds per user per merchant per 24-hour period
- Maximum ₹50,000 in refunds per user per day without manual review

5.2 Merchant Liability for Fraud Chargebacks
Where merchant fraud is established, the acquiring bank is liable to reimburse the customer. Merchants with chargeback ratios above 2% are placed on enhanced monitoring.

SECTION 6: INCIDENT RESPONSE

6.1 Containment SLA
Upon detection of a fraud pattern:
- Accounts must be flagged within 5 minutes
- Step-up authentication must be enforced within 15 minutes
- Account suspension (if warranted) within 30 minutes
- Customer notification within 60 minutes

6.2 Investigation and Documentation
All fraud investigations must be documented with:
- Transaction timeline
- Fraud indicators observed
- Actions taken
- Customer communication log
Retained for minimum 5 years per RBI record-keeping norms.
